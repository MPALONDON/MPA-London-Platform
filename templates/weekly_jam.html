About Our Weekly Jams

Our weekly jam sessions are a safe, welcoming space where musicians and singers of all abilities gather to perform together. These sessions provide an opportunity to:

  • Play with other musicians in a supportive environment
  • Improve your improvisation skills
  • Learn new songs and techniques
  • Network with other musicians
  • Have fun and enjoy making music together

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Jam Night open to all skill levels?

Yes, our jam nights are open to musicians and singers of all abilities. Whether you're a beginner, intermediate player, or advanced musician, you are welcome to participate.

What genres of music are played?

The genres played can vary depending on the preferences of those attending. The beauty of jam nights lies in the diversity of musical styles, creating a collaborative and inclusive musical experience.

What instruments are welcome?

We welcome all instruments including guitars, bass, drums, keys, piano, singers, brass, woodwind, strings, and percussion instruments.

Do I need to bring my own instrument?

While we provide a professional set of backline equipment (drum kit, amplifiers, keyboards, and microphones), you should bring your own instrument if you play one. Drummers should bring their own drumsticks.

Is there a bar or refreshments available?

We have a bar serving a selection of alcoholic and non-alcoholic drinks, as well as snacks to keep you refreshed during the session. Please note that ID will be required for the purchase of alcoholic beverages. We maintain a respectful environment and will not tolerate disorderly behavior - you may be asked to leave if necessary.

How do I get to the venue?

Our venue is located at 30 Commerce Rd, Brentford, London. There are several bus routes that pass directly outside the venue, and we're just a 10-minute journey from the town centre.

Do I need to book in advance?

While drop-ins are welcome, we recommend contacting us in advance to ensure there's space available, especially if you're bringing specific equipment or have any special requirements.

What happens during a typical jam session?

Each session begins with a brief introduction and warm-up. Participants can then suggest songs to play, and we'll form small groups to practice and perform. The session ends with an open mic where everyone can showcase what they've been working on.
